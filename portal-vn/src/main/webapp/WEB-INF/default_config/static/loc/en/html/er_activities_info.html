Title Emissions and removals per activity
Quantity 27 (one per ecoregion and country level for the period 195-2000, 2000-2005, 2005-2010)
Data owner VNFOREST
Data provider UN-REDD Programme (LOA FIPI-FAO No. FAOR 09/2015 and LOA FIPI-FAO No. FAOR 19/2016)
Data producer FIPI consigned under UN-REDD Programme (as instructed by MARD) based on VNFOREST owned data sets.
Data production date 2016 (as deliverable from the LOA FIPI-FAO No FAOR 19/2016, used for the Modified FRL submission to the UNFCCC)
Format and resolution tables based on the spreadsheet 13 to 16 of the Viet Nam Forest Reference (Emissions) Level calculations Excel file
Description of the data Table presenting the emissions (+) or removals (-) in tCO2 associated to REDD+ activity defined in the Forest Reference (Emission) Level submission, and the resulting FREL and FRL at national level.
Remarks The activities are defined as follows:
  • Reducing emissions from deforestation ("Deforestation"): Activity of conversion of forests to non-forest land, i.e. forest cover changes from category 1-12 to 13-17 in the matrix.
  • Reducing emissions from forest degradation ("Degradation"): Activity resulting in a downward shift in terms of carbon stock between forest types, including Evergreen broadleaf forest volume-based sub-types of "rich, medium, and poor" (based on the average standing volume per ha) and other forest types (deciduous, bamboos etc.), i.e. positive changes between the categories 1-12.
  • Enhancement of forest carbon stocks from reforestation ("Reforestation"): Activity of land use change from non-forest land to forest land, changes form the categories 13-17 to 1-12.
  • Enhancement of forest carbon stocks from forest restoration ("Restoration"): Activity resulting in upward shift of carbon stock between forest types, including Evergreen broadleaf forest volume-based sub-types of "rich, medium, and poor" (based on the average standing volume per ha) and other forest types (deciduous, bamboos etc.), i.e. negative changes between the categories 1-12.
