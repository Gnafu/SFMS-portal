Title Carbon stock

Data owner VNFOREST
Data provider UN-REDD Programme (LOA FIPI-FAO No. FAOR 09/2015 and LOA FIPI-FAO No. FAOR 19/2016)
Data producer FIPI consigned under UN-REDD Programme (as instructed by MARD) based on VNFOREST owned data sets.
Data production date 2016 (as deliverable from the LOA FIPI-FAO No FAOR 19/2016, used for the Modified FRL submission to the UNFCCC)
Format and resolution table based on the spreadsheet 06 of the Viet Nam Forest Reference (Emissions) Level calculations Excel file
Description of the data Table representing the average carbon stock (above and belowground biomass) per forest type in tC/ha for each ecoregion and at country level.
Remarks The carbon stock estimates are developed using tree level data collected through the NFIMAP Cycle I to IV, implemented by FIPI every year from 1991 to 2010. Measurements were taken in 1,706 plots, 2,500 plots, 2,750 plots and 2,049 plots respectively for cycle I to IV, based on a systematic sampling design nationwide. Tree diameter at breast height (DBH) was measured for all trees with DBH > 5 cm and three tree height (H) measurements were taken per sub plot. Each tree species was recorded. For all forest types except Mangroves, the carbon stock for each plot was calculated based on the measured DBH, the H simulated with H-DBH relationships and wood densities associated with the species.

Allometric equations developed at national level in Viet Nam are used to estimate the carbon of each tree in Evergreen broadleaf, Deciduous and Bamboo forests. For Plantation and Coniferous trees and forests, volume equations were applied and wood densities used to convert volume to biomass. For other forest types excluding Mangroves, the equations for Evergreen broadleaf forests were used. The tree carbon stock were summed at each subplot and average carbon stocks per ha were calculated per forest type and eco-region. The carbon stock estimates of Mangrove forests are from literature review, applying weighted carbon stock averages for the main species present in the North and Central regions and for the South of Viet Nam.
